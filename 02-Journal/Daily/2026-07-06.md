# 💡 Key Learnings

Today's biggest realization was that Merge and Rebase achieve the same goal—integrating changes—but they do so in different ways.

I learned that:

- Merge preserves the complete history of both branches.
- Rebase creates a cleaner, linear history by replaying commits.
- Merge is generally safer for shared branches.
- Rebase should be avoided on public branches because it rewrites commit history.
- Choosing between Merge and Rebase depends on the team's workflow and collaboration style.
